Adiabactic Process
Happen quickly so there is no heat transfer (Q=0). Work is done and the temperature changes.
The 2nd Law
Perpetual motion machines of the second kind (machines that spontaneously convert thermal energy into mechanical work) are impossible.
Reversed Heat Engine
Does work to transfer energy from a low-temperature reservoir to a high-temperature reservoir.
Refrigerator
The purpose of the refrigerator is to remove heat from the cold reservoir.
Isobaric Process
Pressure stays constant. The heat transferred to the system does work and/orchanges the internal energy of the system.
Heat Pump
The purpose of the heat pump is to supply heat to the high-temperature reservoir.
Coefficient of Performance
This is the ratio of the heat extracted or supplied to the work done by the external agency. It will differ, depending on the ultimate purpose of the engine
Thermal Efficiency
ε = (Pout/Pind)
Pind: indict. power
Pout: output power
Mechanical Efficiency
η = (Pout/Pin)
Pin: input power
Pout: output power
The 1st Law
that energy cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system.
ΔU = ΔQ + ΔW
Isovolumetric Process
Also known as isochoric, this takes place at constant volume. No work is done on or by the system.
The 0th Law
If two systems are in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
Output Power
Also known as the brake power
Pout = Tω
Indicated Power
The theorectical power, i.e. area enclosed in a p-V diagram.
The 3rd Law
The entropy of a system approaches a constant value as the temperature approaches absolute zero.
Isothermal Process
An isothermal process is a change of a system, in which the temperature remains constant: ΔT = 0.
An Ideal Gas
The internal energy is purely kinetic.
A Heat Engine
Extracts energy from its environment in the form of heat and converts it into useful work.
Input Power
The power that is derived from the burning of the fuel.
Thermal Efficiency
The ratio of the maximum useful work that can be done by the engine to the energy supplied by the fuel.
